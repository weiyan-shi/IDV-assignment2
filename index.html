Visualization 1: "How is gay marriage changing in the US?"

Source: Reddit (DataIsBeautiful)

Percentage growth can be misleading: The visualization shows the percentage increase in same-sex marriages per state, but it does not include absolute numbers. This is problematic because a small initial count in 2014 can lead to an exaggerated percentage change. For example, a state with very few same-sex marriages in 2014 might show a 300% increase, even if the actual number of new marriages is quite low. Without knowing the baseline values, the significance of these percentages is unclear.

Negative growth in Iowa (-11%): Iowa is the only state with a reported decline in same-sex marriages, but no context is given. It is unclear whether this is due to people moving out, changes in reporting, or an actual reduction in marriages. This lack of explanation makes the data point seem like an anomaly rather than a meaningful trend.

Missing demographic factors: The map does not account for key factors such as population size, legal changes, or cultural attitudes that could influence marriage rates. For instance, states with larger LGBTQ+ populations might naturally have higher numbers, while states with restrictive laws in the past might show sudden increases due to legalization.

Visualization 2: "Top 50 Universities Under 50 Years for PhD Studies"

Source: LinkedIn (Emmanuel Tsekleves)

Critique:

ðŸ”´ Data Issues

Lack of ranking methodology: The visualization presents a ranking but doesn't explain how these universities were ranked. What metrics were used? Is it based on faculty quality, research output, or student satisfaction?

ðŸŽ¨ Idiom Issues

Cluttered Number Placement: The clustering of multiple numbers in certain regions, particularly in Europe, makes it difficult to distinguish individual institutions. For example, numbers such as "2, 5-6, 8, 19" are grouped together, leading to visual confusion. Additionally, some numbers overlap or are placed too close to each other, as seen in the Asia-Pacific region, further complicating readability.

Unclear purpose of the world map: It is not clear whether the map aims to show the ranking or simply the geographic distribution of universities. The use of numbers forces viewers to cross-reference with the table, making it harder to interpret.

Font size inconsistency: The title is large and bold, but the university names and rankings are relatively small, making readability difficult.

Overuse of blue tones: The entire design uses a monochromatic blue scheme, which reduces contrast. Differentiating regions with different colours could improve clarity.

âœ… Suggestions for Improvement

  • Group universities by region with color-coding
  • Create a clear visual distinction between rankings (perhaps using size or color intensity)
  • Add a heat map overlay to show concentration of young universities
  • Add ranking methodology to explain how universities were selected.
  • Make the world map more informative by either placing university names directly or using colour coding instead of numbers.
  • Use better colour contrast so that the table and map are easier to read.
